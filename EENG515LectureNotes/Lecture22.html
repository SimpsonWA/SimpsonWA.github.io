• Single Value Decomposition (SVD) gives us a way to factor non-square matrices

SVD Fundamentals: Factorization and Terminology

  • Let \(A\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix with real or complex entries (ie any matrix)
  • Then \(A\) can be factored as: $$ A = U \Sigma V^H $$
  • \(U\) is \(m \times m\) and unitary
  • \(\Sigma\) is \(m \times n\) and diagonal

  • \(V\) is \(n \times n\) and unitary

  • Let \(P = min(m,n)\) then \(\Sigma\) has the form (with \(\Sigma_{1} \in \mathbb{R}^{P \times P}\)) if \(P = m \leq n\) $$ \Sigma = \left[ \begin{array}{cccc|ccc} \sigma_{1} & 0 & ... & 0 & 0 & ... & 0 \\ 0 & \sigma_2 & ... & 0 & . & ... & . \\ . & ... & ... & 0 & . & ... & . \\
    0 & 0 & ... & \sigma_p & 0 & ... & 0 \\ \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c|c} \Sigma_{1}& \vec{0}_{m \times(n-m)} \end{array} \right] $$

  • \(\Sigma\) has the form (with \(\Sigma_{1} \in \mathbb{R}^{P \times P}\)) if \(m \geq n =P\) ![[Pasted image 20240927192816.png]]

Terminology:

  • The element \(\sigma_1,\sigma_3,...,\sigma_p\) are known as singular values of \(A\).
  • For any matrix \(A\) the singular values are always real and non-negative and usually ordered: $$ \sigma_{1}\geq \sigma_{2}\geq ... \geq \sigma_{p}\geq 0 $$
  • The columns of \(U\) are known as the left singular values of \(A\)
  • The columns of \(V\) (or rows of \(V^{H}\)) are known as the right singular vectors of \(A\)

  • We have \(A = U \Sigma V^{H}\) so we say \(\vec{y} = A\vec{x} = U \Sigma V^{h} \vec{x}\)

  • For \(i = 1,2,3,...,n\)
