Sum rule and Marginal Probability

  • The probability that \(X\) will take the value \(x_i\) regardless of \(Y\) is written as: $$ P(X = x_i) $$
  • \(P(X = x_i)\) is given by the fraction of the total number of points that fall in column \(i\): $$ P(X = x_{i)}= \frac{c_i}{N} $$
  • Obviously we have \(c_{i}= \sum_{j} n_{ij}\) thus: $$ P(X=x_{i)}= \sum_{j=1}^{L} P(X=x_i,Y=y_j) $$
  • This is called the sum rule
  • \(P(X=x_i)\) is called marginal probability

Product Rule and Conditional Probability

  • If we consider only the instances when \(X=x_i\) then the fraction of such instances for which \(Y = y_j\) is written as \(P(Y=y_{j} | X= x_i)\) and it is called the conditional probability of \(Y=y_j\) given \(X = x_i\) $$ P(Y=y_{j} | X= x_{i)}= \frac{n_{ij}}{c_{i}} $$
  • We can then derive: $$ P(Y=y_{j} , X= x_{i})= \frac{n_{ij}}{c_{i}} = \frac{n_{ij}}{c_{i}} \frac{c_i}{N}=P(Y=y_{j} | X= x_{i})_P(X=x_i) $$
  • This is called the product rule

Bayes' Theorem

  • Bayes' theorem is a combination of the symmetry property \(P(X,Y) = P(Y,X)\) with conditional properties: $$ P(Y|X) = \frac{P(X|Y)*P(Y)}{P(X)} $$
