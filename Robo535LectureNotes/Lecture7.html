Using example

$$ P(mistake) = P(X \in R_{1}, Digit = 2) + P(X \in R_{2}, Digit = 1) = P(X\in R_1,C_{2})+ P(X\in R_{2},C_{1}) $$ - if \(X\) is discrete: $$ P(mistake)= \sum\limits_{X\in R_{1}} P(X,C_{2}) + \sum\limits_{X \in R_{2}} P(X,C_{1}) $$ - if \(X\) is continuous: $$ P(mistake)= \int\limits_{ R_{1}} P(X,C_{2}) + \int\limits_{ R_{2}} P(X,C_{1}) $$ - Assign \(X\) to \(R_k\) to minimize the misclassification rate or increase the correct rate: $$ P(correct) = P(X \in R_{1},C_{1}) + P(X \in R_{2},C_{2}) = \sum\limits_{k=1}^{k} P(X\in R_K,C_k) $$ - For discrete: $$ P(correct) = \sum\limits_{k=1}^{k} \sum\limits_{X \in R_k} P(X,C_k) $$ - For Continuous: $$ P(correct) = \sum\limits_{k=1}^{k} \int_{R_k} P(X,C_{k}) $$

Making Decisions with Loss/Utility Function:

  • So far we have considered each misclassification as equally bad, or each correct classification as equally good; but this isn't applicable in the real world
  • For example imagine a space shuttle launching given all the data what is less risky:
    • Incorrect prediction of damage (no launch) is better than incorrect prediction of no damage (launch, damage)
  • We can describe this using a loss matrix by:

Payoff Matrix:

Utility Matrix (Uk):

- The expected loss of \(X\) is: $$ \sum\limits_{k=1}^{k} L_{kj}P(X,C_k) $$ - Overall expected loss: $$ E(L) = \sum\limits_{k=1}^{k} \sum\limits_{j=1}^{k} \int_{R_{j}}L_{jk} P(X,C_{k}) dx $$

Decision Theory

  • Decision theory is a calculus for decision making under uncertainty which invalues:

    • Set of primitive outcomes: such as \(A,B,...,Flying,Etc\)
    • Preferences on outcomes: \(A \succ B\)
    • Subjective degrees of belief (Probabilities)
    • Lotteries: uncertain outcomes \(L = [P_1,S_1;P_2,S_2;...;P_n,S_n]\) \ ![[Pasted image 20241026142922.png]]

      Axioms of Preference

  • Orderability: Exactly on of \(A \succ B,\ B \succ A\), or \(A \sim B\) holds true

  • Transitivity: if \(A \succ B\) and \(B \succ C\) then \(A \succ C\)
  • Continuity: if \(A \succ B \succ C\) then there exists P such that \([P,A; 1-P,C] \sim B\) ![[Pasted image 20241026143225.png]]
  • Substitutability: if \(A \succ B\) then \([P,A;1-P,C] \sim [P,B;1-P,C]\) ![[Pasted image 20241026144122.png]]
  • Monotonicity: if \(A \succ B\) and \(P>Q\) then \([P,A;1-P,B] \succ [Q,A;1-Q,B]\) ![[Pasted image 20241026144106.png]]
  • Decomposability: \([P,A; 1-P,[Q,B; 1-Q,C]] \sim [P,A;(1-P)Q,B;(1-P)(1-Q),C]\) ![[Pasted image 20241026144243.png]]
